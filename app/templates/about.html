About Pollination Africa 2025

Celebrating All Pollinators for Food Security, Biodiversity and Livelihoods

The Pollination Africa Symposium 2025 is East Africa's premier platform dedicated to advancing pollinator conservation, promoting biodiversity, and strengthening food security across the region.

Hosted in Arusha, Tanzania, this symposium brings together farmers, conservationists, researchers, policymakers, and development partners to harness the power of all pollinators—from bees and butterflies to birds and bats—for sustainable development and economic transformation.

Our Mission

Why Pollinators Matter

Pollinators are the unsung heroes of East African agriculture. From tiny native bees to brilliant sunbirds, these diverse species play a vital role in increasing crop yields, sustaining ecosystems, and supporting rural livelihoods.

Pollination Africa 2025 shines a spotlight on the critical importance of diverse pollinators to farming, the environment, and economic opportunity in the region and beyond.

From the honeybees that pollinate our coffee crops to the butterflies dancing through our flower gardens, from the sunbirds visiting our orchards to the beetles working in the shadows—each pollinator species contributes uniquely to our agricultural success and ecological health.

By bringing together stakeholders from across East Africa and beyond, we're building a movement to protect and celebrate these essential species, ensuring food security and sustainable livelihoods for generations to come.

Our Core Objectives

The symposium is designed to drive real impact across three key pillars:

  • Foster Collaboration. Build regional partnerships and facilitate knowledge exchange among farmers, researchers, conservationists, and policymakers across East Africa and beyond.
  • Showcase Innovation. Highlight cutting-edge technologies, sustainable practices, and market access solutions that support diverse pollinator species and the farmers who depend on them.
  • Drive Policy Impact. Influence agricultural and environmental policies to enhance food security, support pollinator health, and build resilient agribusinesses across the region.

Meet the Pollinators

Beyond Bees: Africa's Diverse Pollinators

From tiny insects to magnificent birds, Africa's pollinators work together to ensure our food security and ecosystem health.

Bees

Honeybees, bumblebees, carpenter bees, and over 1,000 native bee species pollinate crops and wild plants across Africa.

Butterflies & Moths

Lepidoptera species provide essential pollination services, especially for flowers with deep nectar tubes.

Birds

Sunbirds, hummingbirds, and other avian pollinators are crucial for many African flowering plants and crops.

Bats & More

Fruit bats, beetles, flies, and other species complete Africa's diverse pollinator community.
