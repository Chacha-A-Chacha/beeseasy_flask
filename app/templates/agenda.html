Focus Areas

What We'll Cover

The agenda addresses critical topics across the entire pollinator conservation ecosystem

Pollinator Economics & Markets

Market access strategies, value chain development, pricing mechanisms, and trade opportunities for pollinator-dependent products

Technology & Innovation

Digital tools, monitoring systems, habitat mapping technologies, and innovations in pollinator-friendly agriculture

Conservation & Biodiversity

Habitat restoration, native pollinator protection, climate resilience strategies, and ecosystem-based approaches

Policy & Regulation

Policy frameworks, regulatory harmonization, land use planning, and incentive mechanisms for pollinator conservation

Capacity Building

Training programs, knowledge transfer, extension services, and empowering the next generation of pollinator stewards

Regional Collaboration

Cross-border partnerships, knowledge exchange platforms, regional research networks, and coordinated conservation efforts

Agenda FAQs

When will the full detailed agenda be released?

The complete day-by-day agenda with confirmed speakers, session times, and workshop details will be released in stages starting 60 days before the event. Subscribe below to receive immediate updates when new sessions are announced.

Can I attend specific sessions only?

Registration covers access to all sessions during both days. While attendance at individual sessions is optional, we encourage full participation to maximize your learning and networking opportunities. All registered participants will receive the full agenda with session details.

How do I choose which workshop track to attend?

When the detailed agenda is released, you'll receive information about each workshop's content and level. You'll be able to pre-register for specific workshops through your participant portal. Some workshops may have limited capacity and operate on a first-come, first-served basis.

Will sessions be recorded?

Selected sessions including keynotes and major panel discussions will be recorded and made available to registered participants after the event. Workshop sessions are typically not recorded due to their interactive nature.

Can I propose a workshop or presentation topic?

Absolutely! We welcome proposals from practitioners, researchers, and innovators. Please contact us at info@pollinationafrica.org with your proposal including title, abstract, and speaker information.

Are there networking opportunities between sessions?

Yes! We've built in dedicated networking time including structured B2B matchmaking sessions, informal receptions, and exhibition viewing periods where you can connect with fellow attendees, speakers, and exhibitors.

What language will sessions be conducted in?

All main sessions will be conducted in English with Swahili interpretation available for select sessions. Workshop sessions may be offered in both English and Swahili depending on facilitator and participant needs.
